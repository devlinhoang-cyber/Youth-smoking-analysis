Binary classification analysis predicting whether youth have ever used cigarettes showed a highly structured pattern that dominated by tobacco involvement. The decision tree showed TOBFLAG- a general indicator of tobacco exposure- is the single most decisive indicator. TOBFLAG= 0 means non-smoker with 100% purity, while TOBFLAF > 0 are individuals that exposed to tobacco before. This split captures almost all variations in cigarette initiation, underscoring how tobacco experience is the strongest dominant. Random forest plot reinforce tree-plot conclusion. TOBFLAG ranks above all predictors in both Mean Decrease Accuracy and Mean Decrease Gini, this confirm its dominant predictive power. The second variables like RMAAGE (age at first marijuana use) and RMDAYS(days of marijuana use) suggest that marijuana exposure may correlate with cigarette initiation, but it does not rival tobacco involvement. from two models, tobacco exposure alone is sufficient to classify youth smoking status is high accuracy, other variables only add minor refinements to the model.

The multi-class classification model predicted the frequency of youths using cigarette by the determine the number of days smoked in past months. The decision tree used a single- dominant split on CIGMDAY < 6, this clearly separate non-smokers and occasional smokers from frequent ones. Individuals that smoke fewer than 6 days per month were classified as none (76%) or occasional (24%). Though, the occasional category remains underrepresented due to strong class imbalance in dataset. Frequent smokers (smoke more than 6 days a month) were classified as frequent with near-perfect purity (99%). The model highlighted a key insight that recent smoking behavior alone fully determined the predicted smoking category, which reflect the skewed distribution of cigarette among youth.

The regression analysis showed that monthly cigarette users among youth is driven by self-reported smoking frequency. Both CART and boosted model highlighted the same underlying structure. The regression tree splits exclusively on IRCIGFM, which produced a clean and monotonic progression from light smoker to moderate, mid-range smoker to heavy ones. The boost model reinforce with values like PRMJMO (past-month marijuana use) and YFLTMRJ2 (lifetime marijuana use) have some contributions to predictive value, but IRCIGFM still remain as dominant predictor. Together, the two models demonstrate that cigarette use intensity is highly structured and primarily determined by direct smoking behavior with other substance usages as secondary indicators.
